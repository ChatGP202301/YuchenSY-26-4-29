Commercial RO Water Purifier User Manual

Scope and buyer information

This manual applies to commercial reverse osmosis water purifier systems supplied by Yuchen Water for distributors, installers, engineering companies and OEM/ODM buyers. It explains the normal configuration, site preparation, start-up, operation checks, maintenance schedule and after-sales information for projects such as 400G, 600G, 800G and 1200G series units. Final pump, membrane, valve, tank, voltage and packaging details are confirmed according to the customer water report and project requirements.

System configuration

A commercial RO system normally includes pre-treatment, security filtration, high-pressure reverse osmosis separation and electrical control. According to site water quality, the system can be equipped with quartz sand filter, activated carbon filter, softening tank, FRP or stainless steel tanks, automatic control valves, precision filter housings, RO membrane housings, high-pressure pump, pressure gauges, flow meters, conductivity or TDS meter, flushing valve, solenoid valve, low-pressure protection and float switch. Optional UV, ozone, dosing or post-treatment modules can be discussed for OEM/ODM supply.

Installation requirements

Install the equipment on a flat, clean and well-drained indoor floor. Leave space around the machine for cartridge replacement, valve service, membrane removal and electrical inspection. The feed-water pipe, drain pipe and product-water pipe must follow the flow direction shown on the system. Power wiring should be completed by qualified personnel, and the actual voltage and phase must match the nameplate before the machine is energized.

Start-up procedure

Before first operation, flush the pre-treatment filters and security filter until the water is clean. Open the inlet valve, check leakage, release air from filter housings and confirm that all pressure gauges return normally. Start the booster or raw-water pump, then start the RO main machine according to the control panel sequence. Flush the RO membranes first, then slowly adjust product-water and concentrate-water flow according to the design data. Record inlet pressure, pump pressure, product flow, concentrate flow and conductivity during commissioning.

Daily operation

During operation, the operator should monitor inlet pressure, pump pressure, product-water flow, concentrate-water flow, conductivity or TDS value, abnormal vibration, pump noise and leakage. If the machine shows low inlet pressure, dry running risk, unstable voltage, repeated protection alarm or sudden water-quality change, stop the system and inspect the cause before restarting. Stable operating records help buyers plan filter replacement and membrane cleaning.

Maintenance schedule

Replace PP or security filter cartridges when pressure drop increases or according to the project schedule. Backwash sand and activated carbon filters according to the valve program and water quality. Check softener salt level and regeneration condition if a softening tank is installed. RO membranes should be cleaned by CIP when product flow drops, salt rejection decreases or differential pressure becomes high. Use suitable cleaning chemicals only under engineer guidance.

Troubleshooting and after-sales

For low product flow, high conductivity, leakage, pump failure or automatic shutdown, check the inlet water supply, pre-treatment blockage, filter cartridge condition, low-pressure switch, solenoid valve, membrane fouling, pump rotation direction and electrical protection. When contacting Yuchen Water, send the model, voltage, photos, short videos, feed-water report and recent operating data so the engineer can identify the problem quickly and provide replacement parts or service advice.
