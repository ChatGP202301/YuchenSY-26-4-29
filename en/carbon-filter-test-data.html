How buyers should interpret the data

  • Match the tested sample construction, dimensions and media to the product under evaluation.
  • Compare flow, capacity, challenge-water conditions and system configuration before using a result in a purchasing decision.
  • A laboratory test report is not the same as product certification or listing.
  • Supporting documents are released only after email verification and manual business review.

Buyer questions about the test data

Are these carbon filters NSF certified?

No certification or listing is claimed on this page. The excerpts reproduce selected results for specific tested samples and conditions.

Which filter models do these results apply to?

Applicability must be confirmed against the tested sample, media, dimensions, flow and system configuration. A result must not be applied automatically to the whole product range.

Can distributors request supporting documentation?

Yes. Complete the protected request, verify your email and provide enough project information for a manual business review.

What should an OEM buyer provide?

Provide the intended cartridge type, size, media, target flow, market, expected quantity and the claim or evaluation requirement you need to support.
